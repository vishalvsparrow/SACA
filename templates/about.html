Here's what I think about this project, myself, and my project guide:


Vishal Vatnani
I have always believed in keeping things simple. Simplicity is the greatest virtue. But do not misunderstand it with naivety. There is only a subtle distinction between things getting simple and things becoming naive. It takes a heart to discern this difference and rejoice with the existence itself, for the Universe knows that what you have created is not a GTU final year project. It is pure energy. Your project then becomes your own consciousness, and there should be nobody to force you with what it should be like. Many professors have taken a look at my project. Most of them failed to understand what I was doing. For a simple reason, that this project is simple. Often it is for the simple things that we try to find complications in simplicity.
Jay Mehta
Professor Jay has been my project guide and a friend. Freedom is what I expected from this project, and that's exactly what he gave me. It doesn't matter what subject a teacher teaches you. A guide may or may not be interested in your project, because the guides are simply not obligated to do anything according to the system of GTU. However, unlike other people, Professor Jay has been more of a friend to me than a guide. His flexibility with my schedule has amazed me. I can do whatever I want with this project, and he always seems to be constantly excited about it. There is no reason why he wouldn't deserve a mention here.
